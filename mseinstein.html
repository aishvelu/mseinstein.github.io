About.

Young Girls Need STEM!

As many know, the lack of female engineers is very much prevalent nowadays. As girls surpass the age of ten or so, their interest in tech and engineering drastically diminishes. The lack of female mentors is without a doubt one of the primary factors responsible for this trend. Consequently, I as well as my colleague Jenna Allman wish to be these mentors that help mitigate this problem—at least in our community. In addition, being mentors relatively close to their age, we believe will make communication easier and learning more effective.

Timing: 5:30 - 6:30 PM every Monday from July 15 - August 19

Location: Walpole Public Library

Team.

"I have always wanted to become an engineer for as long as I could remember. However, I was discouraged to do so by much of my family. Since I was young, they insisted on me become a doctor, attempting to convince me by alluding to the substantial impact I would make on the world by helping save so many people. However, my mission is to make an impact by my own means and though what I am truly passionate about. The first step in fulfilling this is Ms Einstein. Supporting young girls and helping excite them about a field that so many women fail to embark on is truly fulfilling and just the preface of my impact."

Aishwarya Velu
